# 03. The Case-First Philosophy

The Case is the center of ICOS.

Not the lead.

Not the contact.

Not the hospital.

Not the invoice.

The Case.

## Why Everything Revolves Around the Case

A Case represents a specific treatment journey.

It connects the patient, treatment request, medical condition, destination, hospital referrals, medical reviews, quotes, finance, tasks, timeline, documents, and audit trail.

The Case is where operational reality becomes visible.

## Patients May Have Many Cases

A Patient is the long-term identity.

A Case is one operational journey.

The same patient may return for:

- Cardiac surgery.
- Chemotherapy cycle 2.
- Follow-up review.
- A future treatment.
- A different condition months later.

Treating every lead as a separate permanent patient would lose continuity.

Treating every patient as one static case would lose operational detail.

ICOS separates Patient and Case because real care requires both.

## Knowledge Grows Through Cases

Every Case teaches the organization something.

It may teach which hospital responds quickly.

It may reveal which coordinator handles a region well.

It may show which documents are commonly missing.

It may expose commission risk.

It may improve future hospital recommendations.

The Case becomes the container for learning.

## Operational History Belongs To Cases

Important history must remain attached to the Case:

- Lead creation.
- Partner attribution.
- Hospital registration.
- Medical review.
- AI draft.
- Clinical approval.
- Hospital package.
- MSO request.
- Quote received.
- Patient decision.
- Deposit received.
- Visa started.
- Travel confirmed.
- Treatment.
- Discharge.
- Follow-up.

The Case preserves the story.

Without the Case, the organization remembers fragments.

With the Case, the organization builds intelligence.
