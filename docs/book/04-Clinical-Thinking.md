# 04. Clinical Thinking

Clinical work inside ICOS is not simply document review.

It is the transformation of incomplete patient information into a hospital-ready clinical package.

## Clinical Workflow

The Clinical Decision Center exists to structure the clinical path from raw documents to decision-ready information.

The workflow includes:

- Document completeness review.
- Missing document management.
- AI clinical draft.
- Internal clinical validation.
- Clinical lead approval.
- Hospital package builder.
- Hospital MSO request.
- Hospital MSO received.
- Clinical recommendation.
- Patient preliminary opinion.
- Ready for quotation.

## Medical Review

Medical Review belongs to the Case.

It helps the organization understand whether the patient has enough information for hospital submission, whether documents are missing, whether urgent risks exist, and what specialty or destination may be appropriate.

## AI Second Opinion

AI can help summarize documents, identify missing information, flag risks, suggest questions, and prepare draft clinical reasoning.

But AI is not the clinical authority.

It is a support layer.

## Human Validation

Human validation remains necessary.

Clinical, ethical, financial, and commission-sensitive decisions must not be automated away.

The system can recommend.

The reviewer must decide.

The audit trail must preserve who approved what, when, and why.

## Ethics

Medical tourism requires ethical judgment.

Not every patient should travel.

Not every treatment request is appropriate.

Not every destination is safe or beneficial.

ICOS must support recommendations such as:

- Travel recommended.
- Travel recommended with urgency.
- Travel after local stabilization.
- Seek local treatment first.
- Not recommended for travel.
- Palliative care appropriate.
- Observation recommended.

The system must help the organization protect patients, not only process cases.
