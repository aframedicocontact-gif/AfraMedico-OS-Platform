# 05. Patient Journey

The Patient Journey is larger than travel.

Travel is one stage.

The journey begins when the patient first appears and continues through treatment, discharge, and follow-up.

## Journey Versus Travel

Travel is operational logistics.

Journey is the complete experience.

The journey includes:

- First contact.
- Referral attribution.
- Case creation.
- Document collection.
- Clinical review.
- Hospital communication.
- Quote decision.
- Finance.
- Visa.
- Flight.
- Arrival.
- Admission.
- Treatment.
- Discharge.
- Return home.
- Follow-up.

## Operational Coordination

Every stage needs an owner.

No Case should have no owner.

No Work Item should have no owner.

Every handoff should be visible.

Every next action should be known.

The patient should never be lost between departments.

## Hospital Handover

Hospital handover is one of the most important moments in the journey.

The hospital must receive the right information, the right documents, the right clinical summary, the right questions, and the right context.

Referral protection may happen early.

Medical Review prepares the full clinical package.

Both matter, and they serve different purposes.

## Follow-Up

The journey does not end at treatment.

Follow-up preserves care quality, patient trust, provider learning, and organizational memory.

Follow-up also teaches the organization which providers deliver strong outcomes, which coordinators communicate well, and where future workflows can improve.
